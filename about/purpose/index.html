Purpose

OpenCode @ MIT is a student organization at MIT that aims to help MIT students to learn more about software and the importance of open source development.

  • Give members an opportunity to learn software development within a team.
  • Advance open source culture within MIT and the local community.
  • Bring helpful open source software to the MIT community and the world.

What can you gain from us?

You will have a chance to work on and learn about many different technologies at Open Code @ MIT, which bring you many opportunities at MIT and in your future career.

  • Meeting people who are passionate about software.
    • Learning from people who are experienced in making software.
    • Discussing technology and thinking about making it better.
  • Exposure to various technologies for all technology stacks.
    • Opportunities with web, desktop, mobile, and other kinds of applications.
  • Working on useful and interesting projects.
    • Add more skills and showcases to your resume.
    • Get mentorship from or mentor fellow projectmates.

What kind of software are we interested in?

  • Open and libre
    • The source code of the software and other relevant components (e.g. music and art) should be available for everyone.
    • Licenses must allow others to use, study, modify, and distribute the work done for free.
    • “Copy-left”, or licenses that require any derivative work to remain open source and libre, are highly encouraged. This allows software to stay open and for future work to contribute to the community.
  • Respects user privacy
    • Collecting data must be voluntary and explicitly communicated to the user and should only be done when necessary.
    • User privacy and security should be high in the priorities.
  • Accessible
    • Highly encouraged to be accessible for people with visual or physical impairments.
    • Highly encouraged to be translatable into different languages and still match the original design.
  • Responsible
    • Must not encourage others to engage in malicious or harmful activities.
    • Must not have a negative impact on the environment or the world.
